My favourite books

  • The Song of Ice and Fire by George R. R. Martin

    As a fantasy fan, how can I not metion one of the densest, beautifly written, delicately complicated tomes that is The Song of Ice and Fire. Popularized by HBO and the mini series (except for the last season, that doesn't count), this magnum opus quickly gained fame and an avid reader base. Here is the link for the George R.R. Martin website where you can read up on him, his books, and books he's been reading in the moments of peace and quiet of not writting Winds of Winter.

  • The Silmarillion and The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

    Need I say more?
    You can read up on Tolkien on the Tolkien Society website

  • David and Goliath by Malcolm Gladwell

    I've found that authors like Malcolm Gladwell give a new perspective on "known" things. For instance, the book David and Goliath have made me question a lot of things we gain and "solid information". Just the fact that you can read him very easily makes him and his ideas aproachable. This is my favourite book in the genre.
    David and Goliath is a good entry book into this soft self-help genre, and if you're interested hit the link to his personal website.
